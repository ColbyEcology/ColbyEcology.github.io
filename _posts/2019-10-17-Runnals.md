In the seventh week of the semester, Ecology Lab students visited Runnals hill to collect data on how tree communities, biomass, and microclimatic conditions change across forest edges. Once at the edge of the forest on the southwest part of campus, they created a transect perpendicular to the edge, and sampled from 15 m into the field, the edge 15 m, and every 15 m until 90 m into the forest interior.

We *luckily* were spared from the nor'easter that struck earlier that morning. By the time we headed to the hill, winds were down, it wasn't raining, and we had a gorgeous view.

Here are a few photos from the day:
